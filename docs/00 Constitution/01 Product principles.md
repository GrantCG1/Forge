# Purpose

The Product Principles define the fundamental beliefs that guide every decision made within Forge.

They are intended to remain stable over time and provide a consistent framework for evaluating features, user experiences and future product direction.

If a proposed feature conflicts with these principles, the feature should be reconsidered or redesigned.

---

# Principle 1 — Life Comes First

### Why it matters

Healthy living should support life, not become its centre.

People have careers, families, relationships, celebrations, setbacks and responsibilities. Forge exists to strengthen these parts of life rather than compete with them.

### What it means for Forge

Forge should fit naturally into a person's life. It should adapt to changing circumstances and never encourage unhealthy obsession or perfectionism.

---

# Principle 2 — Educate, Adapt and Assist

### Why it matters

Forge exists to do more than provide plans.

It teaches users why recommendations are made, adapts those recommendations when circumstances change, and assists when time, motivation or mental capacity are limited.

### What it means for Forge

Every significant feature should educate, adapt, assist, or ideally support all three.

---

# Principle 3 — The User Remains in Control

### Why it matters

Confidence is built through ownership.

Forge should support decision-making without replacing it.

Recommendations empower people far more effectively than instructions.

### What it means for Forge

Forge recommends, explains and guides.

The user always makes the final decision.

---

# Principle 4 — Reduce Cognitive Load

### Why it matters

Healthy decisions often become difficult when people are busy, tired or overwhelmed.

Technology should reduce unnecessary effort while preserving meaningful choice.

### What it means for Forge

Forge should simplify decisions, organise information and reduce decision fatigue without removing opportunities for learning or creativity.

---

# Principle 5 — Build Independence

### Why it matters

The greatest success of Forge is creating users who gradually need it less.

Education should increase confidence and capability rather than dependency.

### What it means for Forge

Every recommendation should leave users slightly more knowledgeable and capable than before.

Forge succeeds when users confidently make good decisions on their own.

---

# Principle 6 — Progress Over Perfection

### Why it matters

Long-term health is built through consistent, sustainable decisions rather than flawless execution.

Life is unpredictable, and healthy living must remain resilient through changing circumstances.

### What it means for Forge

Forge should never punish users for setbacks.

Instead, it should help them make the best possible decision from where they are today.

---

# Principle 7 — Learn Through Living

### Why it matters

People learn most effectively through experience.

Education should feel like a natural part of everyday life rather than a separate activity.

### What it means for Forge

Forge should embed learning into recipes, workouts and recommendations so that knowledge develops naturally through regular use.

---

# Principle 8 — Stewardship

Forge encourages users to care for their bodies, minds and lives as gifts entrusted to them. Health is pursued not for vanity or comparison, but as faithful stewardship that enables people to serve others and live life well.

---

# Applying the Principles

Every meaningful product decision should be evaluated against these principles.

Questions to consider include:

* Does this feature help people live healthier lives without becoming the centre of their lives?
* Does it educate, adapt or assist?
* Does the user remain in control?
* Does it reduce unnecessary cognitive load?
* Does it help users become more independent?
* Does it encourage sustainable progress rather than perfection?
* Does it naturally develop the user's understanding?

If the answer to these questions is largely "no", the feature should be reconsidered.

---

# Closing Statement

The principles of Forge are intended to guide decisions, not restrict innovation.

As Forge evolves, these principles may be refined through experience and learning.

However, every refinement should strengthen the original purpose of Forge:

**To empower people to live healthier, more balanced lives through understanding, adaptability and thoughtful assistance.**
