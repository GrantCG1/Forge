# Purpose

The Forge Product Charter defines the identity, purpose and boundaries of Forge.

It exists to ensure that every future feature, design decision and technical implementation remains aligned with Forge's long-term vision.

When uncertainty arises, this document should provide the framework against which decisions are evaluated.

---

# Product Identity

Forge is a lifestyle companion that educates, coaches and assists people in living healthier, more balanced lives.

Rather than attempting to optimise every aspect of a user's health, Forge seeks to reduce unnecessary cognitive load while preserving personal autonomy.

Forge exists to help people make better decisions more consistently, while gradually increasing their understanding and confidence so that healthy living becomes simpler, more sustainable and more enjoyable.

---

# Mission

Forge exists to educate, assist and empower people to live healthier, more balanced lives.

By reducing the mental effort associated with everyday health decisions, Forge enables people to spend less time planning and more time living.

---

# Vision

Forge aims to become the most trusted lifestyle companion for people seeking sustainable health rather than perfection.

Success is measured not by how dependent users become on Forge, but by how confidently they are able to make healthy decisions themselves.

---

# Target Users

Forge is designed primarily for people who wish to live full, balanced and healthy lives without allowing health itself to become an obsession.

These individuals often enjoy cooking, exercise, learning and personal growth, but they value flexibility, spontaneity and balance equally.

They are not necessarily training for elite athletic performance, nor do they seek perfection.

Instead, they wish to become healthier versions of themselves while maintaining rich and fulfilling lives outside of health and fitness.

Forge supports users across different stages of their journey, adapting recommendations as goals, circumstances and capabilities evolve.

---

# Problems Forge Solves

Forge seeks to reduce the friction associated with healthy living.

It helps users by:

- Reducing decision fatigue.
- Providing trustworthy recommendations.
- Explaining the reasoning behind recommendations.
- Educating users through everyday decisions.
- Adapting to changing circumstances.
- Supporting consistency rather than perfection.
- Making healthy choices easier without removing user control.

---

# Core Design Philosophy

Forge is built upon six foundational beliefs.

## 1. Health is an enabler, not the objective.

The purpose of health is to enable people to enjoy life more fully.

Forge therefore optimises for living well rather than pursuing health metrics for their own sake.

---

## 2. Every recommendation should reduce cognitive load.

Healthy decisions should become easier, not more complicated.

Forge should simplify decisions while preserving meaningful choice.

---

## 3. The recommendation is never the only path.

Forge always provides a recommended starting point.

However, users should always be free to:

- Explore alternatives.
- Adjust recommendations.
- Learn more.
- Challenge themselves.
- Make different choices.

Forge assists rather than dictates.

---

## 4. Education should happen naturally.

Education should accompany decisions rather than interrupt them.

Users should always understand:

- Why something is recommended.
- What alternatives exist.
- What trade-offs different choices involve.

---

## 5. Adaptability is more valuable than optimisation.

The objectively "best" decision is often not the most appropriate one.

Forge adapts recommendations according to:

- Available time.
- Current goals.
- Experience.
- Injuries.
- Motivation.
- Personal preferences.
- Changing circumstances.

---

## 6. Balance creates sustainability.

Forge does not pursue perfection.

It seeks sustainable habits that improve quality of life over many years.

---

# Product Promises

Forge makes the following commitments to every user.

Forge will:

- Explain why recommendations are made.
- Preserve meaningful user choice.
- Adapt to changing circumstances.
- Educate without overwhelming.
- Optimise for balanced living.
- Encourage consistency over perfection.
- Respect the user's autonomy.
- Support long-term wellbeing above short-term optimisation.

---

# Product Boundaries

Forge intentionally avoids becoming a product centred around obsession, comparison or unhealthy optimisation.

Forge is **not** designed primarily for:

- Elite performance optimisation.
- Competitive comparison.
- Social status.
- Guilt-driven behaviour.
- Perfectionism.
- Health anxiety.

Forge should never encourage users to measure their worth through health metrics alone.

---

# Automation Philosophy

Forge may prepare.

Forge may suggest.

Forge may organise.

Forge should rarely act without explicit user confirmation.

Whenever meaningful decisions are made on behalf of a user, Forge should request permission and provide sufficient context for informed approval.

Automation exists to reduce effort, not remove agency.

---

# Success Criteria

Forge succeeds when users:

- Make healthier decisions with less effort.
- Understand why those decisions matter.
- Feel more confident managing their health.
- Experience greater balance across work, health and leisure.
- Become more capable of making good decisions independently.
- Feel supported rather than controlled.

---

# Non-Goals

Forge is not intended to become:

- A calorie counting application.
- A social competition platform.
- An influencer ecosystem.
- A guilt-based habit tracker.
- A replacement for qualified healthcare professionals.
- A product that seeks to maximise every measurable health metric.

---

# Decision Framework

Future decisions should be evaluated against the following questions.

1. Does this reduce cognitive load?
2. Does this preserve meaningful user autonomy?
3. Does this educate the user?
4. Does this encourage balanced living?
5. Does this support long-term sustainability?
6. Does this align with Forge's mission and vision?

If the answer to any of these questions is **no**, the feature or decision should be reconsidered.

---

# Guiding Statement

> **Forge exists to make healthy living simpler, more understandable and more sustainable—so that people can spend less time managing their health, and more time living their lives.**
